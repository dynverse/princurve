Benchmarking princurve

Robrecht Cannoodt

princurve 2.1 contains major optimisations if the approx_points parameter is used. This is showcased on a toy example, where the number of points was varied between 102 and 106.

We can see princurve 2.1 scales linearly w.r.t. the number of rows in the dataset, whereas princurve 1.1 scales quadratically. This is due to the addition of the approximation step added in between the smoothing and the projection steps (explained in more detail in benchmarks.md).
